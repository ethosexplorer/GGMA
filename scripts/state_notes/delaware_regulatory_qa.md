# Delaware OMM / OMC — Regulatory Q&A Reference
Business Source: https://omb.delaware.gov/marijuana-commissioner/
Patient Source: https://dhss.delaware.gov/dhss/dph/hsp/medmarhome.html

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** Medical Marijuana Act (2011) and Delaware Marijuana Control Act (Adult-Use, 2023).
- **Program Type:** DUAL (Medical and Recreational/Adult-Use)
- **Regulatory Bodies:** 
  - **Office of the Marijuana Commissioner (OMC)** — oversees the new adult-use rollout and overall policy.
  - **Office of Medical Marijuana (OMM)** under the Division of Public Health (DHSS) — manages the medical patient registry and existing compassion centers.
- **Patient Registry Contact:** 855-420-6797, MedicalMarijuanaDPH@delaware.gov
- **Business Licensing Contact:** OMC@delaware.gov

## Latest News & Key Issues
- **Adult-Use Rollout:** Delaware legalized adult-use in 2023. The OMC is actively developing the adult-use licensing framework (applications expected to open in late 2024).
- **Qualifying Conditions Removed:** Delaware recently removed its strict list of qualifying conditions. Any physician can now recommend medical marijuana for any condition they believe will benefit the patient.
- **Self-Certification for Seniors:** Patients 65 and older can self-certify for a medical card without needing a physician's recommendation.

## License Types (Adult-Use Framework)
The Delaware Marijuana Control Act outlines specific licensing categories and strict caps:
1. **Retail Licenses** (Capped at 30, with 15 reserved for social equity)
2. **Cultivation Licenses** (Capped at 60, with 20 reserved for social equity and 20 for microbusinesses)
3. **Product Manufacturing Licenses** (Capped at 30, with 10 for social equity and 10 for microbusinesses)
4. **Testing Laboratory Licenses** (Capped at 5, with 2 reserved for social equity)

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist (Upcoming Adult-Use)
1. **Application Windows:** Licenses will be issued during specific application periods (expected Q3/Q4 2024).
2. **Scoring System:** Applications will be competitively scored.
3. **Social Equity & Microbusinesses:** A significant portion of the strict license caps are reserved specifically for social equity applicants and microbusinesses.
4. **Current Medical Centers:** Existing Medical Compassion Centers will have pathways to convert or expand into the adult-use market.
5. Apply via OMC portal (currently in development): https://omb.delaware.gov/marijuana-commissioner/

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **State-Level Processing:** Applications are processed by the OMM under the DHSS.
2. **Qualifying Conditions:** None specifically required anymore. Left to physician discretion.
3. **Process:**
   - Meet with a DE-licensed MD, DO, APRN, or PA (unless 65+).
   - If the physician is registered in the state system, the application can be submitted entirely online.
   - If the physician is not registered, the patient must mail a physical paper application.
   - Proof of DE residency required (DE Driver's License or ID).
   - Pay the state fee: $50 for 1 year, $75 for 2 years, $100 for 3 years.
4. **Benefits of State Card:** Immediate access (adult-use stores are not open yet) and likely tax exemptions once the adult-use market opens.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

- **Q: Are there limits on how many business licenses will be issued?**
  A: Yes, Delaware has strict, hard caps written into law (e.g., exactly 30 retail licenses, 60 cultivation licenses).

- **Q: Do seniors need a doctor's note for a medical card?**
  A: No, Delaware residents aged 65 and older may self-certify and do not require a physician's signature on their application.

- **Q: How much is the patient application fee?**
  A: $50 for a one-year card. Patients can also opt for 2-year ($75) or 3-year ($100) cards.

- **Q: Does Delaware accept out-of-state medical cards (Reciprocity)?**
  A: No, Delaware does not have formal reciprocity agreements. Out-of-state patients must register with the Delaware system to purchase at local compassion centers.

## Key Compliance Notes
- **Strict License Caps:** Unlike states with open licensing, Delaware will be extremely competitive due to the hard caps (30 total retailers for the state).
- **Self-Certification:** Unique to DE, agents must inform patients 65+ that they do not need to pay a doctor for a consultation.
- **Testing Requirements:** Rigorous testing is managed by OMM/OMC. All compassion centers must test products before retail.
