# Alabama AMCC — Regulatory & Application Reference
Source: https://amcc.alabama.gov/
Businesses: https://amcc.alabama.gov/cannabis-business-applicants-2/
Patients: https://amcc.alabama.gov/patients/

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** SB 46 (Darren Wesley 'Ato' Hall Compassion Act), signed May 2021
- **Regulatory Body:** Alabama Medical Cannabis Commission (AMCC)
- **Contact:** P.O. Box 309585, Montgomery, AL 36130
- **Program Type:** Medical Only

## License Types (6 categories)
1. **Integrated Facility** — cultivate + process + dispense (33 applicants)
2. **Cultivator** — grow only (12 applicants)
3. **Processor** — manufacturing only (11 applicants)
4. **Dispensary** — retail only (17 applicants)
5. **Secure Transporter** — licensed transport (9 applicants)
6. **State Testing Laboratory** — quality testing (3 applicants)

## Qualifying Conditions (Patients)
- Autism Spectrum Disorder
- Cancer-related cachexia, nausea, pain
- Crohn's Disease
- Depression
- Epilepsy / seizure disorder
- HIV/AIDS
- Panic Disorder
- Parkinson's Disease
- Persistent nausea (not related to pregnancy)
- PTSD
- Sickle Cell Anemia
- Spasticity / chronic pain from spinal cord injury
- Terminal illness
- Tourette Syndrome
- Condition causing chronic/intractable pain

## Registered Certifying Physicians
- 33 registered physicians across Alabama (as of 5/13/2026)
- Must be registered with AMCC to certify patients
- Registration expires 12/31/2026
- Specialties: Family Medicine, Internal Medicine, Pain Management, Psychiatry, Ophthalmology, General Medicine

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. Determine license type: Integrated Facility, Cultivator, Processor, Dispensary, Secure Transporter, or Testing Lab
2. Apply through official AMCC application process
3. Submit required supplemental documents per license category
4. Pay applicable licensing fees
5. All applications and exhibits become public record
6. Applicant may redact confidential/proprietary information
7. Must cite statutory authority for any redactions (§ 36-12-40 et seq., Code of Alabama 1975)
8. AMCC reserves right to revise applicant redactions
9. Applicant agrees to defend any public records exemption claims
10. Await AMCC review and commission vote

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. Confirm patient has a qualifying medical condition (see list above)
2. Schedule evaluation with an AMCC-registered certifying physician
3. Physician evaluates patient and provides medical cannabis certification
4. Patient registers through the AMCC patient registry system
5. Receive AMCC patient registration card
6. **Caregiver Registration** (if applicable):
   - Must be 21+ years old
   - Complete caregiver application through AMCC
   - Link to registered patient's account
   - Background check required
7. Patient may purchase from licensed Alabama dispensaries only
8. Products available: tablets, capsules, tinctures, patches, suppositories, nebulizers, topicals (NO smoking/vaping flower)

### 👨‍⚕️ PROVIDER (Physician) Registration Checklist
1. Must hold active Alabama medical license (MD or DO)
2. Must be in good standing with Alabama Board of Medical Examiners
3. Apply for AMCC Certifying Physician registration
4. Complete required AMCC cannabis medicine training/certification
5. Submit registration application with specialty and practice info
6. Provide primary office address, county, and contact number
7. Registration valid through 12/31 of current cycle (renewable annually)
8. Once registered, physician may:
   - Evaluate patients for qualifying conditions
   - Issue medical cannabis certifications
   - Monitor patient treatment plans
9. Must maintain patient records per AMCC guidelines
10. Report adverse events to AMCC as required

### ⚖️ Key Compliance Notes
- All applications and exhibits are public records under Alabama law
- Confidential/proprietary info can be redacted by applicant
- AMCC not obligated to agree with applicant's grounds for redaction
- Products restricted — NO smokable or vapeable flower products allowed
- Only AMCC-registered physicians may certify patients
