## License Types (Dual-Track: Medical + Recreational)

### Medical (AMMA — Prop 203)
1. **Nonprofit Medical Marijuana Dispensary** — patient sales
2. **Dispensary Agent** — employee of dispensary
3. **Lab / Lab Agent** — independent testing
4. **Qualifying Patient** — patient registry card
5. **Designated Caregiver** — authorized patient caregiver

### Recreational (Smart & Safe Act — Prop 207)
1. **Marijuana Establishment** — retail sales (adult-use)
2. **Marijuana Establishment Agent** — employee card
3. **Marijuana Testing Facility** — independent testing
4. **Marijuana Event License** — temporary events

## Key Regulations
- **Dual-Use:** Arizona has both medical AND recreational programs operating simultaneously
- **Age:** 21+ for recreational; no age minimum for medical (patients under 18 require caregiver)
- **Possession (Rec):** Up to 1 oz of marijuana (max 5g concentrates)
- **Home Grow:** Up to 6 plants per person, 12 per household max
- **Delivery:** Permitted since Nov 1, 2024
- **No Residency Requirement** for patient registry
- **Dispensary Allocation:** AZDHS manages annual dispensary certificate allocation (2026 cycle active)
- **Testing Required:** All products must pass laboratory testing
- **Fingerprinting:** Required for all applicants (dispensary agents, caregivers, etc.)
- **Electronic ID Cards:** All cards now issued electronically via AZDHS portal
- **Statutes:** ARS Title 36, Chapter 28.1 (medical) + ARS Title 36, Chapter 28.2 (recreational)
- **Rules:** AAC Title 9, Chapter 17

## Debilitating Medical Conditions (Medical Program)
- Cancer
- Glaucoma
- HIV/AIDS
- Hepatitis C
- Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)
- Crohn's Disease
- Agitation of Alzheimer's Disease
- Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
- Chronic or debilitating disease producing: cachexia/wasting, severe chronic pain, severe nausea, seizures (incl. epilepsy), severe/persistent muscle spasms (incl. MS)

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. Determine license type: Medical Dispensary vs. Recreational Establishment
2. Check AZDHS 2026 dispensary certificate allocation availability
3. Medical dispensaries must be organized as Arizona nonprofit entities
4. Recreational establishments must comply with Prop 207 zoning requirements
5. Secure premises location — verify local zoning ordinances
6. Apply via AZDHS **Facility Licensing Portal**: https://facility-licensing.azdhs.gov/
7. Submit fingerprint clearance cards for ALL principals/board members/agents
8. Complete AZDHS background check process
9. Submit operating plan per AAC Title 9, Chapter 17
10. Demonstrate adequate security measures (surveillance, alarm, access control)
11. Provide proof of financial capability
12. Obtain local government approval/zoning compliance documentation
13. Pay application fees (check AZDHS current fee schedule)
14. Complete MLMS Training: https://www.azdhs.gov/licensing/marijuana/index.php#mlms
15. **Dispensary Agent Cards:** All employees must have valid dispensary/establishment agent cards
16. Agent card application via Individual Licensing Portal
17. Agent cards require: fingerprinting + background check + photo
18. Complaints filed via: https://facility-licensing.azdhs.gov/s/MarijuanaFacilityComplaints
19. Lab testing facility requires independent operation — cannot hold dispensary license
20. **Tax compliance:** 5.6% sales tax + 16% excise tax on recreational sales

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **Adult Patients (18+):**
   - Must have qualifying debilitating medical condition (see list above)
   - Obtain physician certification of qualifying condition
   - Apply online via AZDHS Individual Licensing Portal: https://individual-licensing.azdhs.gov/
   - Submit application + physician certification + photo ID
   - Pay $150 application fee (reduced fee available for SNAP recipients)
   - Receive electronic registry ID card
   - Card valid for 2 years; renewable
2. **Patients Under 18:**
   - Must have qualifying debilitating medical condition
   - Requires custodial parent/legal guardian as designated caregiver
   - Caregiver controls acquisition, dosage, and frequency
   - Both patient and caregiver applications required
3. **Designated Caregiver:**
   - Must be 21+ years old
   - Apply via Individual Licensing Portal
   - Fingerprint clearance card required
   - Background check required
   - Limited to assisting one patient at a time (some exceptions)
   - May cultivate on behalf of patient if patient qualifies for home cultivation
4. **Cultivation Rights:**
   - Patients may cultivate at home IF residence is 25+ miles from nearest dispensary
   - Cultivation Boundary Check tool available on AZDHS website
   - Max 12 plants in enclosed, locked facility
5. **Renewal:**
   - Must renew before expiration via online portal
   - Physician recertification required at renewal
6. **Clinical Trials:** AZDHS maintains clinical trial program information
7. **Card Verification:** Employers and law enforcement can verify cards via AZDHS portal

### 👨‍⚕️ PROVIDER (Physician) Registration Checklist
1. Must hold active Arizona medical license (MD or DO)
2. Must be licensed by Arizona Medical Board or Board of Osteopathic Examiners
3. Review ARS Title 36, Chapter 28.1 certification requirements
4. Physician evaluates patient for qualifying debilitating medical condition
5. Issues written certification if condition is met
6. Certification requirements:
   - Establish bona fide physician-patient relationship
   - Review patient's medical history
   - Conduct in-person examination
   - Document qualifying condition in patient chart
   - Certify that benefits outweigh risks
7. Certification submitted to AZDHS via patient's application
8. Physician may certify any number of patients
9. **No separate AZDHS cannabis-specific physician registration required** (unlike Alabama)
10. Must maintain medical records per Arizona Medical Board standards
11. Cannot have financial interest in dispensary
12. AZDHS Physician FAQ: https://www.azdhs.gov/licensing/medical-marijuana/#faqs-physicians
13. Report concerns: AZDHS complaint process available

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

### General
- **Q: Is marijuana legal in Arizona?**
  A: Yes. Medical (since 2010, Prop 203) and recreational (since 2020, Prop 207) are both legal. Adults 21+ can possess up to 1 oz. Patients with qualifying conditions can register for medical cards.

- **Q: Can I grow marijuana at home?**
  A: Recreational: Yes, up to 6 plants per person, 12 per household, in enclosed locked space. Medical: Only if residence is 25+ miles from nearest dispensary.

- **Q: Can I get marijuana delivered?**
  A: Yes, delivery has been legal since November 1, 2024.

- **Q: What are the taxes on recreational marijuana?**
  A: 5.6% state sales tax + 16% excise tax = 21.6% total.

- **Q: Can cities/towns opt out?**
  A: Cities and towns can regulate time, place, and manner but cannot fully prohibit licensed establishments under Prop 207.

### Patients
- **Q: How do I apply for a medical marijuana card?**
  A: Online via the Individual Licensing Portal at individual-licensing.azdhs.gov. You need physician certification, photo ID, and the application fee.

- **Q: What is the application fee?**
  A: $150 standard; reduced rates for SNAP recipients.

- **Q: How long is my card valid?**
  A: 2 years from date of issuance.

- **Q: Do I still need a medical card if recreational is legal?**
  A: Medical cards offer benefits: lower taxes, higher possession limits, cultivation rights within 25-mile boundary, and access to higher-potency products.

### Business
- **Q: What type of entity is required for a dispensary?**
  A: Medical dispensaries must be Arizona nonprofit entities. Recreational establishments follow Prop 207 requirements.

- **Q: Do I need separate licenses for medical and recreational?**
  A: Existing medical dispensaries can apply for dual-use authorization under Prop 207.

- **Q: What tracking system does Arizona use?**
  A: Arizona uses its own state-managed tracking system through AZDHS (not METRC).

### Providers
- **Q: Do I need special certification to recommend medical marijuana?**
  A: No separate state cannabis certification. Any licensed AZ physician (MD/DO) can certify patients for qualifying conditions.

- **Q: Can I certify patients via telehealth?**
  A: Check current AZDHS rules — telehealth policies may have been updated post-COVID.

## Key Compliance Notes
- All license applications processed through AZDHS online portals
- Electronic ID cards — no physical cards mailed
- Fingerprinting required for agents, caregivers, and dispensary applicants
- All products must be independently lab-tested before sale
- Recreational packaging must be child-resistant and opaque
- No public consumption allowed
- DUI laws fully apply — ARS 28-1381
- Federal prohibition still applies — marijuana remains Schedule I
- Expungement available for eligible prior convictions (petition-based)
- Barcode scanner integration required for dispensaries
