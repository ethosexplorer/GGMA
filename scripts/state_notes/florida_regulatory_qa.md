# Florida OMMU — Regulatory Q&A Reference

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** Amendment 2 (Medical, 2016). Adult-use (Amendment 3) failed on the 2024 ballot.
- **Program Type:** MEDICAL ONLY.
- **Regulatory Body:** **Office of Medical Marijuana Use (OMMU)** under the Florida Department of Health.
- **Patient Registry Contact:** 800-808-9580, MedicalMarijuanaUse@flhealth.gov
- **Business Licensing Contact:** OMMU Professionals division.

## Latest News & Key Issues
- **Adult Use Failure:** Florida remains heavily restricted to medical-only after the failure of the adult-use ballot initiative.
- **Strict Vertical Integration:** Florida is one of the strictest vertically integrated markets in the U.S. A single business (Medical Marijuana Treatment Center - MMTC) must cultivate, process, and dispense the product.
- **Seasonal Residents:** Florida uniquely allows "seasonal residents" (snowbirds) to obtain a medical card if they live in FL for at least 31 consecutive days each year.

## License Types
1. **Medical Marijuana Treatment Center (MMTC):** The ONLY license type available in Florida. It is fully vertically integrated. There are no standalone cultivation, processing, or retail licenses.

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist (MMTC)
1. **Extremely High Barrier to Entry:** Application windows are notoriously rare and usually triggered only by increases in the state patient registry population (e.g., 1 new MMTC license for every 100,000 active patients).
2. **Vertical Integration:** The applicant must demonstrate the financial and operational capacity to cultivate, process, transport, and dispense cannabis.
3. **Application Fee:** The non-refundable application fee is extremely high (often exceeding $146,000 when windows open).
4. **Approval Process:** Subject to rigorous background checks, financial disclosures, and operational plan reviews.

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **State-Level Processing:** Handled directly by the OMMU through the Medical Marijuana Use Registry (MMUR).
2. **Qualifying Conditions:** Cancer, Epilepsy, Glaucoma, HIV/AIDS, PTSD, ALS, Crohn's Disease, Parkinson's, Multiple Sclerosis, or medical conditions of the same kind or class.
3. **Process:**
   - Must be diagnosed by an OMMU-qualified physician IN-PERSON (telehealth not allowed for initial visit).
   - Physician enters the patient into the MMUR.
   - Patient receives login credentials via email to apply online.
   - Patient uploads Proof of Residency (FL Driver's License/ID, or seasonal resident docs like a lease).
   - Submit the $75 processing fee to the state.
4. **Approval:** Online applications take ~10 business days. Approved patients receive an email that serves as a temporary card until the physical card arrives.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

- **Q: Can I apply for a standalone dispensary license in Florida?**
  A: No. Florida requires full vertical integration. You must be licensed as an MMTC, which controls the entire supply chain.

- **Q: How much does a patient card cost?**
  A: The state fee is $75 annually, plus whatever the qualified physician charges for the consultation.

- **Q: Can seasonal residents get a medical card?**
  A: Yes. A seasonal resident who lives in Florida for at least 31 consecutive days can apply by providing two forms of proof (like a utility bill or lease) instead of a FL Driver's License.

- **Q: Is there patient reciprocity in Florida?**
  A: No. Florida does not accept out-of-state medical marijuana cards. Out-of-state residents cannot purchase from MMTCs.

## Key Compliance Notes
- **Vertical Integration:** Agents must immediately disqualify business leads looking for "retail only" or "grow only" in Florida unless they have tens of millions in backing to acquire an existing MMTC.
- **Telemedicine Ban:** Initial physician exams must be conducted in person.
- **Background Checks for Caregivers:** Non-close relatives applying as caregivers must complete a Level 2 background screening.
