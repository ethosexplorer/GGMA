# Colorado MED / CDPHE — Regulatory Q&A Reference
Business Source: https://sbg.colorado.gov/med
Patient Source: https://cdphe.colorado.gov/medical-marijuana
Licensing FAQ: https://sbg.colorado.gov/med/licensing

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** Amendment 20 (Medical, 2000) and Amendment 64 (Retail, 2012).
- **Program Type:** DUAL (Medical and Recreational/Adult-Use)
- **Regulatory Bodies:**
  - **Marijuana Enforcement Division (MED)** under the Dept of Revenue — business licensing and enforcement.
  - **Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment (CDPHE)** — state patient registry.
- **Patient Registry Contact:** 303-692-2184, medical.marijuana@state.co.us
- **MED Contact:** dor_medenquiries@state.co.us

## Latest News & Key Issues
- **Social Equity Program:** Colorado has a robust social equity program, reducing application fees and providing grants for eligible applicants.
- **Hospitality Licenses:** Colorado now issues Marijuana Hospitality and Hospitality & Sales licenses (social use venues).
- **Online Only:** MED has transitioned almost entirely to online applications via the MED portal.

## License Types
1. **Medical and Retail Stores** (Dispensaries)
2. **Cultivation Facilities** (Tiered by plant count, e.g., Tier 1: 1,800 plants up to Tier 5: 13,800 plants)
3. **Products Manufacturing**
4. **Testing Facilities**
5. **Transporters**
6. **Marijuana Hospitality Business** (allows consumption on-site)
7. **Retail Marijuana Hospitality and Sales** (allows sale and consumption on-site)

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. **Local Approval:** Like CA, businesses must receive local municipality approval. Dual licensing structure.
2. **Finding of Suitability:** All owners must submit to a background check and financial review BEFORE applying for a business license.
3. **Premises Diagram:** Detailed, to-scale diagram of the facility.
4. **Security Plan:** Alarm, surveillance, and limited access areas.
5. **Fees:** Application fee plus an annual license fee (varies by type/tier).
6. Apply via the MED Online Portal: https://sbg.colorado.gov/med/online-services

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **State-Level Processing:** Applications are submitted directly to the state (CDPHE) via an online portal.
2. **Qualifying Conditions:** Cancer, Glaucoma, HIV/AIDS, Cachexia, Persistent Muscle Spasms, Seizures, Severe Nausea, Severe Pain, PTSD, Autism Spectrum Disorder, or any condition for which a physician prescribes an opioid.
3. **Process:**
   - Provider certification from a licensed MD or DO. (Cannot be an advanced practice nurse or PA).
   - Valid Colorado ID or Driver's License.
   - Social Security Number.
   - Apply online through the CDPHE portal.
   - Fee: $29.50 (fee waivers available for indigent patients).
4. **Benefits of State Card:** Lower age limit (18+ instead of 21+), access to higher potency products, exemption from the 15% state retail marijuana sales tax, and higher possession limits (up to 2 ounces).

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

- **Q: Can I get a MED license without local approval?**
  A: No, you must have both state and local approval to operate.

- **Q: How much can a medical patient purchase in Colorado?**
  A: Medical patients can purchase up to 2 ounces of flower, 20,000mg of THC in infused products, and 8 grams of concentrate per day.

- **Q: What tracking system does Colorado use?**
  A: METRC. Colorado was the first state to implement METRC.

- **Q: What are the taxes in Colorado?**
  A: Retail cannabis has a 15% state sales tax and a 15% excise tax on the wholesale transfer. Medical cannabis is EXEMPT from the 15% retail sales tax (only standard state sales tax of 2.9% applies).

## Key Compliance Notes
- **Owner Suitability:** A major hurdle. Financials, taxes, and background must be pristine.
- **Hospitality Licenses:** A growing sector, but highly restricted by local zoning.
- **METRC:** Colorado's integration with METRC is mature; agents should emphasize our tracking parity.
