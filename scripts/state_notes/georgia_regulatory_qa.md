# Georgia GMCC / DPH — Regulatory Q&A Reference
Business Source: https://www.gmcc.ga.gov/
Patient Source: https://dph.georgia.gov/low-thc-oil-registry

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** Haleigh’s Hope Act (2015) and Georgia’s Hope Act (2019).
- **Program Type:** LOW-THC OIL ONLY (Maximum 5% THC). Not a full medical cannabis or adult-use program.
- **Regulatory Bodies:**
  - **Georgia Access to Medical Cannabis Commission (GMCC)** — oversees business licensing (production and dispensing).
  - **Georgia Department of Public Health (DPH)** — oversees the patient Low THC Oil Registry.
- **Patient Registry Contact:** (770) 909-2765, THCRegistry@dph.ga.gov
- **Business Licensing Contact:** rules@gmcc.ga.gov

## Latest News & Key Issues
- **Extremely Limited Licensing:** Georgia has only licensed a handful of companies to produce and dispense Low-THC oil. Applications are currently closed.
- **Form Factor Restrictions:** Only Low-THC oil is permitted (up to 5%). Flower, edibles, and vaporization of raw plant material are strictly prohibited.

## License Types
1. **Class 1 Production License:** Limited to 2 licensees statewide. Allows growing indoors in facilities up to 100,000 square feet.
2. **Class 2 Production License:** Limited to 4 licensees statewide. Allows growing indoors in facilities up to 50,000 square feet.
3. **Dispensing License:** Allows licensed producers to open a limited number of dispensaries to sell the Low-THC oil directly to registered patients.

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. **Application Status:** Currently CLOSED. Georgia issued its initial competitive licenses and is not accepting new applicants for production.
2. **Dispensing Expansion:** Only the companies holding Class 1 or Class 2 Production Licenses are eligible to apply for Dispensing Licenses. Standalone dispensaries are not permitted for outside applicants.
3. **Barriers to Entry:** The market is entirely closed to new entrants.

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **State-Level Processing:** Managed by the DPH Low THC Oil Registry.
2. **Qualifying Conditions:** Cancer, ALS, Seizure disorders, MS, Crohn's Disease, Mitochondrial Disease, Parkinson's, Sickle Cell, Tourette's, Autism (severe), Epidermolysis Bullosa, Alzheimer's, AIDS, Peripheral Neuropathy, Intractable Pain, and PTSD.
3. **Process:**
   - Patient CANNOT register themselves directly.
   - Must consult with a physician licensed in Georgia.
   - Patient and physician complete the **Low THC Oil Waiver** form (must be notarized).
   - Physician enters the certification into the secure DPH registry.
   - Patient pays the $25 fee.
   - The DPH notifies the patient when the card is ready to be picked up at a designated public health office.
4. **Benefits of State Card:** Protects patients from criminal prosecution for possessing up to 20 fluid ounces of low-THC oil.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

- **Q: Can a client apply for a retail dispensary in Georgia?**
  A: No. Dispensing licenses are restricted ONLY to the six companies that hold Class 1 and Class 2 production licenses.

- **Q: What forms of cannabis are legal in Georgia?**
  A: Only Low-THC oil (maximum 5% THC content). Raw flower, edibles, and traditional high-THC products are illegal.

- **Q: How does a patient get their Low THC Oil card?**
  A: The physician must submit the application on the patient's behalf. The patient then picks up the physical card at a designated local Public Health Office.

- **Q: Does the GMCC issue the patient cards?**
  A: No. The GMCC oversees the businesses. The Department of Public Health (DPH) oversees the patients.
