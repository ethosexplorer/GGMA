# Hawaii DOH — Regulatory Q&A Reference
Business Source: https://health.hawaii.gov/medicalcannabis/
Patient Source: https://health.hawaii.gov/medicalcannabisregistry/

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** Act 228 (2000) for patients, Act 241 (2015) established the dispensary system.
- **Program Type:** MEDICAL ONLY. Adult-use efforts have repeatedly stalled.
- **Regulatory Body:** **Hawaii Department of Health (DOH)** oversees both the Medical Cannabis Registry Program and the Medical Cannabis Dispensary Program.
- **Patient Registry Contact:** (808) 733-2177, medicalcannabis@doh.hawaii.gov
- **Business Licensing Contact:** medicalcannabis@doh.hawaii.gov

## Latest News & Key Issues
- **Out-of-State Patient (OSP) Reciprocity:** Hawaii allows out-of-state patients to apply for a temporary 329 card to purchase while visiting the islands.
- **Condition Removal (Upcoming):** As of July 1, 2025, Hawaii will no longer require patients to be diagnosed with a strict list of qualifying conditions.
- **Strict Caps:** Dispensary licenses are highly restricted and vertically integrated. There are only a handful of licensed dispensaries across all islands.

## License Types
1. **Medical Cannabis Dispensary License:** Fully vertically integrated. Allows the licensee to operate production centers (cultivation/manufacturing) and a limited number of retail dispensing locations.

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. **Closed Market:** Hawaii has strict statutory caps on the number of dispensary licenses per county. Applications are rarely open.
2. **Vertical Integration Required:** Licensees must cultivate, manufacture, and dispense their own products.

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **State-Level Processing:** Managed by the Hawaii DOH via the ehawaii.gov portal.
2. **Process (In-State Resident):**
   - Meet with a Hawaii-licensed M.D., D.O., or A.P.R.N.
   - Patient creates an account at `medmj.ehawaii.gov`.
   - Patient completes the online application, uploads ID (HI driver's license/ID or passport), and pays the $38.50 fee ($35 state fee + $3.50 portal fee).
   - Physician reviews and submits the certification to the DOH.
   - Card is issued digitally (electronic 329 card).
3. **Process (Out-of-State Patient / 329 OSP):**
   - Valid for 60 days (can apply twice per calendar year).
   - Can apply up to 60 days before arriving in Hawaii.
   - Must upload their valid home-state medical cannabis card AND home-state ID. (International cards not accepted).
   - Application fee is $49.50.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

- **Q: Can a tourist purchase medical cannabis in Hawaii?**
  A: Yes, if they hold a valid medical cannabis card from another US state or territory, they can apply for a temporary Hawaii "329 OSP" card online before their trip.

- **Q: How much does the registry card cost?**
  A: $38.50 for in-state residents (1-year or 2-year depending on physician certification) and $49.50 for out-of-state temporary cards.

- **Q: Are there standalone grow operations in Hawaii?**
  A: No, the market is strictly vertically integrated.

- **Q: Is adult-use legal in Hawaii?**
  A: No. Cannabis is for registered medical patients only.

## Key Compliance Notes
- **Reciprocity Process:** Hawaii's reciprocity is NOT automatic. Out-of-state patients MUST register and pay the fee to the Hawaii DOH to get a 329 OSP card before entering a dispensary.
- **Digital Cards:** Hawaii no longer mails physical cards; patients download their 329 card directly to their phones.
