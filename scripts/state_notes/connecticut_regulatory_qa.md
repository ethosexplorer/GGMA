# Connecticut DCP — Regulatory Q&A Reference
Business Source: https://portal.ct.gov/cannabis/knowledge-base/articles/licensing/licensing-home-page
Patient Source: https://portal.ct.gov/DCP/Medical-Marijuana-Program/Medical-Marijuana-Program
Adult Use Legalization: R.S.A. 21-1 (2021)

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** P.A. 12-55 (Medical, 2012) and S.B. 1201 (Adult-Use, 2021).
- **Program Type:** DUAL (Medical and Recreational/Adult-Use)
- **Regulatory Body:** **Department of Consumer Protection (DCP)** — Medical Marijuana Program (MMP) and Adult-Use division.
- **Patient Registry Contact:** dcp.mmp@ct.gov
- **Business Licensing Contact:** dcp.cannabis@ct.gov

## Latest News & Key Issues
- **Social Equity Council (SEC):** Connecticut places a heavy emphasis on Social Equity. Half of all new licenses are reserved for Social Equity applicants.
- **Lottery System:** Many non-equity license types are distributed via a lottery system managed by the DCP.
- **Fee Elimination:** CT eliminated the $100 state registration fee for medical patients starting July 1, 2023.

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. **Social Equity Verification:** If applying as an SEC applicant, must submit an application to the SEC for verification of income and residency.
2. **Lottery Entry:** For applicable license types, businesses must enter the lottery during open application windows.
3. **Provisional License:** Lottery winners are granted a provisional license and have 14 months to build out their facility.
4. **Final License Requirements:** 
   - Local zoning approval
   - Detailed security and operational plans
   - Background checks via the DESPP (Dept. of Emergency Services and Public Protection)
   - Final inspection by the DCP
5. Apply via the eLicense Portal: https://www.elicense.ct.gov/

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **State-Level Processing:** Applications are submitted directly to the DCP via the DAS BizNet portal.
2. **Qualifying Conditions:** Cancer, Glaucoma, Parkinson's, MS, Epilepsy, Cachexia, Wasting Syndrome, Crohn's, PTSD, Sickle Cell, Tourette's, Intractable Headache, and others.
3. **Required Forms:**
   - Provider certification (submitted directly by the physician into the BizNet system).
   - Patient registration completed by the patient online.
4. **Process:**
   - Visit a certified practitioner (Physician, PA, or APRN).
   - Provider submits certification online.
   - Patient creates an account on the **DAS Business Network (BizNet)**.
   - Patient uploads Proof of Identity (CT Driver's License or ID) and Proof of CT Residency (Utility bill, lease).
   - Since July 2023, the state fee has been eliminated ($0).
5. **Benefits of State Card:** Exempt from adult-use cannabis taxes (approx 20%), higher possession limits, access to higher potency products, and priority access during shortages.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

- **Q: How does a patient change their designated dispensary?**
  A: Patients can log into their BizNet account and change their preferred dispensary facility once every 24 hours.

- **Q: What tracking system does Connecticut use?**
  A: Connecticut utilizes BioTrackTHC for its statewide seed-to-sale tracking system.

- **Q: Are there application fees for medical patients?**
  A: No, as of July 1, 2023, Connecticut eliminated the $100 registration fee for medical patients.

- **Q: Does a business need local zoning approval before entering the lottery?**
  A: No. Unlike California, applicants do NOT need local zoning approval to enter the CT lottery. However, they MUST obtain it before their provisional license can be converted to a final active license.

## Key Compliance Notes
- **BizNet vs eLicense:** Patients use BizNet; Businesses use eLicense.
- **Lottery System:** Agents must be aware that the state uses a lottery system, meaning immediate licensure is not guaranteed unless acquiring an existing license.
- **Social Equity:** CT has one of the strictest social equity requirements in the country. Income and residency history are heavily scrutinized.
