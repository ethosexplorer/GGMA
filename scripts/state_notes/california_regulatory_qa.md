# California DCC / CDPH — Regulatory Q&A Reference
Business Source: https://www.cannabis.ca.gov/

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** Compassionate Use Act of 1996 (Prop 215) and Adult Use of Marijuana Act of 2016 (Prop 64). Consolidated under MAUCRSA.
- **Program Type:** DUAL (Medical and Recreational/Adult-Use)
- **Regulatory Bodies:**
  - **Department of Cannabis Control (DCC)** — all business licensing and compliance
  - **California Department of Public Health (CDPH)** — state patient registry (MMICP)
  - **California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA)** — taxes
- **Patient Registry Contact:** 916-552-8666, mmpinfo@cdph.ca.gov
- **DFA/DCC Contact:** 1-844-61-CA-DCC, info@cannabis.ca.gov

## Latest News & Key Issues

### ⚠️ Local Municipality Control (High Volume Rule Variations)
- Unlike most states, California operates under a strict dual-licensing system.
- **A business MUST obtain local authorization (city/county permit) BEFORE applying for a state DCC license.**
- Over 60% of California's 482 cities currently BAN retail cannabis sales.
- Agents must advise all intake businesses to verify their exact local jurisdiction ordinances first.

### Provisional Licensing Phase Out
- California has phased out provisional licenses. All new applicants must meet CEQA (California Environmental Quality Act) requirements to obtain an annual license.

## License Types
DCC issues dozens of specific license types grouped into:
1. **Cultivation** (Indoor, Outdoor, Mixed-light, Nursery, Processor) - sized from Specialty Cottage to Medium.
2. **Manufacturing** (Type 7 Volatile, Type 6 Non-volatile, Type N Infusions, Type P Packaging)
3. **Retail** (Storefront and Non-Storefront/Delivery)
4. **Distribution** (Type 11 and Type 13 Transport-only)
5. **Testing Laboratories** (Type 8)
6. **Microbusiness** (Must engage in at least 3 of: cultivation, manufacturing, distribution, retail)
7. **Cannabis Event Organizers**

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. **Local Approval:** Must secure local city/county permits FIRST.
2. **CEQA Compliance:** Complete environmental impact review.
3. **Surety Bond:** $5,000 bond required.
4. **Premises Diagram:** Detailed floor plans, camera placements, and limited access areas.
5. **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs):** Detailed security, inventory, quality control, and transport procedures.
6. **Live Scan Fingerprinting:** Required for all owners.
7. **Labor Peace Agreement (LPA):** Required for businesses with 20+ employees.
8. Apply via DCC Licensing Portal (Accela): https://aca6.accela.com/DCC/Welcome.aspx

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. **County-Level Processing:** California does not process patient cards directly at the state level. Patients MUST apply in person through their local County Health Department.
2. **Qualifying Conditions:** Anorexia, Arthritis, Cachexia, Cancer, Chronic Pain, HIV/AIDS, Glaucoma, Migraines, Severe Nausea, Seizures, or any other debilitating condition approved by a physician.
3. **Required Forms:**
   - CDPH 9042 (Application Form)
   - CDPH 9044 (Written Documentation of Patient's Medical Records / Physician Recommendation)
4. **Process:**
   - Get recommendation from a licensed California physician (MD or DO).
   - Gather proof of identity (CA ID/Driver's License) and proof of county residency (utility bill, lease).
   - Schedule appointment with local county health department.
   - Pay county fee (capped at $100, or $50 for Medi-Cal beneficiaries).
5. **Benefits of State MMIC:** Exempts patient from the 15% state cannabis excise tax and standard sales tax. Also allows possession of up to 8 oz of dried flower.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

- **Q: Can I get a DCC license without local approval?**
  A: NO. You must have explicit local authorization before DCC will issue a state license.

- **Q: How much can a medical patient purchase?**
  A: State MMIC holders can possess up to 8 ounces of dried flower and up to 6 mature or 12 immature plants.

- **Q: What tracking system does California use?**
  A: California uses METRC (Marijuana Enforcement Tracking Reporting Compliance) for the state's California Cannabis Track-and-Trace (CCTT) system.

- **Q: What are the taxes in California?**
  A: 15% state excise tax on the gross receipt of retail sales, plus state sales tax (7.25% - 10.75%), plus any local cannabis business taxes (which can range from 1% to 15%). Medical patients with a state-issued MMIC are exempt from the sales tax.

## Key Compliance Notes
- **CEQA Compliance:** Massive hurdle for cultivators and manufacturers.
- **Labor Peace Agreement:** Mandatory for 20+ employees.
- **Microbusiness:** A highly popular license type allowing vertical integration (3 out of 4 core activities).
- **Delivery:** Delivery is permitted statewide, even in cities that ban retail storefronts.
