# Alaska AMCO — Regulatory Q&A Reference
Source: https://www.commerce.alaska.gov/web/amco/MarijuanaFAQs
Medical: https://health.alaska.gov/media/ocjibnfh/medicalmarijuana.pdf

## Program Overview
- **Legal Basis:** AS 17.38, passed by citizen initiative Nov 4, 2014
- **Regulatory Body:** Marijuana Control Board (MCB), under AMCO
- **Tracking System:** METRC (Franwell) — real-time RFID/barcode seed-to-sale
- **Contact:** marijuana.licensing@alaska.gov | (907) 269-0350
- **Office:** 550 W 7th Ave, Suite 1600, Anchorage, AK 99501
- **Hours:** Mon-Fri 9am-4pm (closed Wed for in-person, closed noon-1pm)

## License Types (6 total)
1. **Retail Marijuana Store** — consumer sales
2. **Standard Marijuana Cultivation Facility** — full-scale grow
3. **Limited Marijuana Cultivation Facility** — small-scale grow
4. **Marijuana Product Manufacturing Facility** — edibles/products
5. **Marijuana Concentrate Manufacturing Facility** — extracts
6. **Marijuana Testing Facility** — independent, cannot hold other license types

## Key Regulations
- **Residency Required:** All persons with financial interest must be AK residents (PFD eligible)
- **No License Cap:** State has no limit; local govts can restrict time/place/manner/number
- **Premises-Based:** Must secure location BEFORE applying; need deed or lease
- **Buffer Zone:** 500 ft from schools, youth/recreation centers, religious buildings, correctional facilities
- **No Delivery:** Not permitted under AS 17.38 or 3 AAC 306
- **No Dispensary Term:** Alaska uses "Retail Marijuana Store" — not "dispensary"
- **Multiple Licenses OK:** Except testing facilities (must be independent)
- **Application Timeline:** Plan for 4-6 month process
- **Local Opt-Out:** Communities can opt out by ordinance or petition election (3 AAC 306.200)
- **METRC Required:** All licensees must use METRC; internet access mandatory

## Personal Use Rules
- **Age:** 21+ only
- **Possession:** Up to 1 oz for personal use
- **Home Grow:** 6 plants max (3 mature/flowering) per person
- **Per Dwelling:** 12 plants max (6 mature) regardless of residents
- **No Cooperative Grows:** Cannot combine limits or proxy for others
- **Transport:** 1 oz or less; can give 1 oz to another adult

## Commercial Transport
- **No Weight Limit** for licensed transfers between businesses
- **Must Be Accompanied** by person with valid Marijuana Handler Permit
- **Sealed/Locked:** Must be in sealed package, locked storage compartment
- **Cannot Ship Unaccompanied** (no goldstreak/mail)

## Handler Permits
- Take approved course → bring certificate + photo ID to AMCO → $50 fee
- Required for all marijuana establishment employees

## Intake Application Setup

### 🏢 BUSINESS Application Checklist
1. Determine license type (see 6 types above)
2. Secure premises location — must have deed or lease BEFORE applying
3. Check local zoning — contact local government (AMCO cannot advise on zoning)
4. Verify 500 ft buffer zone compliance (schools, youth centers, churches, correctional)
5. Create myAlaska account + obtain Alaska business license number
6. Initiate application online at AMCO website
7. Post Public Notice at premises + conspicuous nearby location for 10 days
8. Notify local government(s) + community council (Anchorage/Mat-Su)
9. Run newspaper ad in local paper, once per week for 3 consecutive weeks
10. Obtain publisher's affidavit from newspaper after ad completes
11. Complete all supplemental forms specific to license type (Form MJ series)
12. Draft operating plan per 3 AAC 306.020(c)
13. Submit fingerprint cards for ALL persons listed (taken within last 12 months via DPS-approved agency)
14. Submit payment — check/money order only to "State of Alaska" (NO credit cards)
15. List ALL persons with direct/indirect financial interest (must be human persons)
16. ALL affiliates must meet Alaska residency requirement (PFD eligible under AS 43.23)
17. AMCO review → deemed complete → 60-day local government protest period
18. 30-day public objection period (completed applications posted on AMCO website)
19. Board consideration within 90 days of completion
20. **Plan for 4-6 month total timeline**
21. After approval: METRC credentialing → inspection → license issued
22. All employees must have valid Marijuana Handler Permit ($50, approved course required)

### 🩺 PATIENT / CAREGIVER Application Checklist
1. Alaska Medical Marijuana Program governed by AS 17.37
2. Patient must have qualifying condition certified by physician
3. **Qualifying Conditions:** Cancer, glaucoma, HIV/AIDS, cachexia, severe pain, severe nausea, seizures (including epilepsy), persistent muscle spasms (including MS)
4. Patient applies through Alaska Department of Health medical marijuana registry
5. Physician provides written certification of qualifying condition
6. Submit registry application with physician certification
7. Receive Alaska medical marijuana patient card
8. **Caregiver Registration:**
   - Must be 21+ years old
   - Apply through Department of Health registry
   - Background check required
   - Linked to specific patient
9. Patient may use licensed retail stores OR grow personal use (6 plants, 3 mature)
10. AS 17.38 legalization does NOT change AS 17.37 medical registry privileges
11. Medical info: https://health.alaska.gov/media/ocjibnfh/medicalmarijuana.pdf

### 👨‍⚕️ PROVIDER (Physician) Registration Checklist
1. Must hold active Alaska medical license
2. Must be in good standing with Alaska State Medical Board
3. Review AS 17.37 medical marijuana statute requirements
4. No separate AMCO registration required (unlike Alabama)
5. Physician evaluates patient for qualifying condition
6. Issues written certification if condition is met
7. Certification submitted by patient to Department of Health registry
8. Provider responsibilities:
   - Establish bona fide physician-patient relationship
   - Document qualifying condition in patient's medical record
   - May recommend but not prescribe (federal classification)
   - Monitor ongoing treatment
9. Cannot hold financial interest in a marijuana establishment (testing facility exception applies per 3 AAC 306.610)
10. Contact: Alaska Department of Health for registry procedures

### ⚖️ Key Compliance Notes
- **METRC mandatory** for all commercial licensees — internet access required
- All license applications are public record
- Misrepresentation of material fact → denial/suspension/revocation
- No delivery to consumers permitted
- No cooperative/communal grows allowed
- DUI still applies — marijuana remains controlled substance under Title 11
- Pesticides regulated by AK Dept of Environmental Conservation (https://dec.alaska.gov/eh/pest/)
- Local communities can opt out entirely via ordinance or petition election
- Handler Permits: approved course + certificate + photo ID + $50 to AMCO
