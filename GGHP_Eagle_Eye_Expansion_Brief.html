◆ Executive Summary

The $56B Industry with No Eyes

The U.S. cannabis industry operates under the strictest surveillance requirements of any legal business sector. Every licensed facility must maintain 24/7 camera coverage. Every transport vehicle must log GPS routes. Every gram must be tracked from seed to sale. Every lab result must be cryptographically verified.

Yet no platform exists to correlate these mandated data streams into a unified operational picture. State regulators check cameras one facility at a time. Transport manifests are cross-referenced manually. Weight discrepancies are discovered weeks after the fact. Diversion goes undetected until audits surface it months later.

Eagle Eye is the answer — a lawful, consent-based, enterprise-grade situational awareness platform that fuses every legally mandated data source into a single AI-correlated intelligence layer. It transforms L.A.R.R.Y from a compliance scanning engine into a full-spectrum sensor fusion AI.

When a dispensary, grow operation, processor, or transporter receives a state license, they sign agreements consenting to: facility inspections, camera access, inventory audits, GPS tracking, financial review, and regulatory reporting. Eagle Eye doesn't surveil anyone — it correlates data that licensees are already legally required to produce.

◆ Platform Architecture

Four-Layer Intelligence Stack

Eagle Eye operates as four distinct layers within the existing GGHP-OS infrastructure, each with its own data sources, processing logic, and access controls.

Layer 1 — Universal Data Ingestion

Open Source & Authorized Sensor Connectors

A universal ingestion framework that accepts any open-source or authorized data input — not limited to cannabis. The connector architecture is plug-and-play: if a data source has an API, feed, stream, or file export, Eagle Eye can ingest it.

  • Regulatory APIs — Metrc, BioTrack, Leaf Data, ABCC (alcohol), DEA ARCOS, FDA REMS, TTB (tobacco)
  • Camera Feeds — RTSP/ONVIF/HLS from facility cameras, municipal traffic cameras, body cams, dashcams
  • GPS / AVL / IoT Telemetry — fleet vehicles, asset trackers, cold chain sensors, environmental monitors
  • GIS / Geospatial — facility locations, grow boundaries, exclusion zones, parcel data, zoning overlays
  • Lab & Testing — COA feeds, potency/purity results, batch linkage, environmental testing
  • License / Registry — state license databases, federal registrations, professional credentials, SEC filings
  • OSINT / Public Records — business filings, court records, news feeds, social media, regulatory actions, patent filings
  • LPR / Vehicle ID — license plate recognition at checkpoints, weigh stations, facility entry/exit
  • Aerial / Satellite — drone imagery, satellite overlays, agricultural compliance imagery
  • Traffic / DOT Feeds — authorized municipal camera networks, highway patrol, corridor monitoring
  • Financial Streams — POS transactions, Care Wallet closed-loop data, SAR patterns, cash flow analytics
  • Custom REST / Webhook / MQTT / Kafka — any system with an API or data export can be connected
Layer 2 — Correlation Engine (L.A.R.R.Y Enhanced)

AI-Powered Sensor Fusion & Pattern Recognition

L.A.R.R.Y evolves from a compliance scanner into a full-spectrum correlation AI that links events across time, space, and data sources.

  • Cross-source event matching — "Transport left Facility A at 2:14 PM but didn't arrive at Facility B until 6:42 PM on a 45-minute route"
  • Temporal correlation (Recency Index patent) — when did events happen relative to each other?
  • Geospatial anomaly detection — route deviations, unauthorized stops, exclusion zone violations
  • Weight/volume discrepancy analysis — "Manifest says 50 lbs left, Metrc says 47 lbs arrived"
  • Financial pattern recognition — unusual transaction patterns, cash flow anomalies
  • Camera AI — motion detection, occupancy counting, after-hours activity alerting
  • Predictive risk scoring — 90-day lookahead on compliance failures, license renewals, violation trends
Layer 3 — Visualization & Command

Role-Based Situational Awareness Dashboards

Every stakeholder sees exactly what they're authorized to see — nothing more, nothing less. Built on the existing GGHP-OS role-based access control system.

  • State Authority Dashboard — statewide compliance heatmap, facility status, active alerts
  • Enforcement Dashboard — live transport tracking, investigation tools, field verification
  • Founder/CEO Command Center — full operational picture with revenue + compliance overlay
  • Public Health Dashboard — product safety trends, contamination tracking, recall coordination
  • Municipal Traffic Dashboard — transport corridor monitoring, route compliance
  • Federal/DEA Dashboard — anonymized aggregate analytics respecting state sovereignty
Layer 4 — Audit & Integrity (SINC)

Immutable Evidence Chain

Every query, every correlation, every alert, every access event is cryptographically logged. Court-admissible by design.

  • Cryptographic audit trails — tamper-evident, timestamped, hash-chained
  • Chain-of-custody documentation — from sensor to screen to courtroom
  • Access logging — who saw what, when, why, and under what authorization
  • Zero-knowledge compliance proofs — verify compliance without exposing raw data
  • Data retention policies — aligned with state regulatory requirements per jurisdiction

◆ Expansion Module

Traffic Surveillance & Transport Intelligence

A standalone revenue stream that extends Eagle Eye beyond cannabis into general public safety infrastructure.

Transport Corridor Monitoring

Route Compliance & Deviation Alerting

Monitors licensed cannabis transport vehicles in real-time against approved routes. Detects unauthorized stops, route deviations, extended travel times, and exclusion zone violations. Alerts enforcement immediately upon anomaly detection.

Municipal Traffic Integration

City DOT Camera Feed Ingestion

Integrates with authorized municipal traffic camera networks to monitor transport corridors. Provides traffic-aware routing recommendations, congestion impact on delivery windows, and visual verification of vehicle location at intersections.

License Plate Recognition

Fleet Vehicle Identification

LPR cameras at facility entry/exit points automatically verify that arriving vehicles match the transport manifest. Flags unknown vehicles, expired registrations, and vehicles not associated with any active license.

Incident & Accident Correlation

Event Response Intelligence

When a transport vehicle is involved in an accident, theft, or diversion event, Eagle Eye automatically correlates: camera feeds near the incident, GPS track history, manifest contents, driver credentials, and chain-of-custody status. Generates a court-ready incident package within minutes.

Revenue Opportunity — Traffic Module

The traffic surveillance module sells independently to municipal DOTs, highway patrol divisions, and city traffic departments — customers who have no interest in cannabis but need sensor fusion for general traffic intelligence. This expands Eagle Eye's addressable market beyond cannabis into the $18B+ public safety technology market.

Pricing: $100K–$500K per jurisdiction annually, or $8K–$25K/month per corridor monitored.

◆ Universal Ingestion Framework

Monitor Any Open Source Input

Eagle Eye is not locked to cannabis data sources. Its universal connector architecture ingests any authorized, open-source, or publicly available data stream — making it expandable to any regulated industry or public safety use case.

Public Records

Government & Regulatory Databases

Automated ingestion of public business filings (Secretary of State), court records (PACER, OSCN), professional license databases, property records, UCC filings, SEC/EDGAR filings, and federal register notices.

News & Media Feeds

Real-Time Media Monitoring

RSS feeds, news APIs, press release wires, industry publications. L.A.R.R.Y correlates news events ("Dispensary raided in Tulsa") with internal data ("That facility had a risk score of 94 last week").

Social & OSINT

Open Source Intelligence

Public social media posts, business review sites, forum discussions, job postings ("dispensary hiring armed security" = risk signal). All public, all legal, all correlated.

Weather & Environmental

Climate & Conditions Data

NOAA weather feeds, air quality indexes, UV exposure data, soil moisture sensors. Critical for outdoor grow compliance, harvest timing verification, and environmental impact monitoring.

IoT & Sensor Networks

Connected Device Ingestion

MQTT/Kafka streams from facility environmental sensors (temperature, humidity, CO2), cold chain monitors during transport, smart lock access logs, and energy consumption patterns.

Custom API / Webhook

Bring Your Own Data Source

Any system with a REST API, webhook, file export (CSV/JSON/XML), or streaming protocol can be connected. Clients and government agencies add their own data sources through a self-service connector builder.

Why Universal Ingestion Matters

A platform locked to one industry's data sources is a product. A platform that can ingest any authorized data stream and correlate it through AI is an operating system. Eagle Eye's universal ingestion framework means:

→ State agencies can add their own cameras, sensors, and databases without custom development
→ Federal agencies can pipe in classified sources through secure connectors
→ MSOs can integrate their existing POS, ERP, and security systems
→ Municipal governments can connect traffic, zoning, and public safety feeds
→ New regulated industries (alcohol, pharma, hemp, firearms) can onboard without rebuilding the platform
